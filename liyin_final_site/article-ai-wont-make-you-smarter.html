AI Won’t Make You Smarter

AI amplifies the quality of your questions, frameworks, and judgment.

AI amplifies the quality of your questions, frameworks, and judgment.

Why this matters

This matters because many people expect AI to remove the need to think. In practice, it often does the opposite. It exposes whether you actually understand the problem you are trying to solve.

What most people get wrong

The biggest mistake is treating AI like a substitute for thought. That creates polished nonsense quickly. If your questions are vague, your assumptions are weak, or your framework is missing, AI can only accelerate confusion.

My framework

My framework is: AI is best used as a mirror, a compressor, and a multiplier. It mirrors the clarity of your thinking back to you. It compresses the time needed to gather, structure, and compare information. And it multiplies the usefulness of a good framework when you already know what you are trying to understand.

What this means for young people

For young people, this means AI is not an excuse to skip the hard part. It is a tool for doing the hard part faster and with more feedback. Use it to stress-test ideas, summarize what you already grasp, generate comparisons, or identify gaps — but do not outsource your judgment.

My current conclusion

My current conclusion is simple: AI will not make you smarter by default. It will make your current level of thinking more visible. Used well, that is an advantage. Used poorly, it is just faster confusion.
